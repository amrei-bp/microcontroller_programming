Interactive: Explore the micro:bit

Scroll through the diagram below to explore every component on the BBC micro:bit V2 — front and back. Each step zooms in on a different part and explains what it does.

The components at a glance

The numbers below match the numbered labels on the images above.

Front

  1. Buttons A and B — physical inputs you can press, separately or together
  2. LED display — 25 red LEDs for text, numbers, and icons; doubles as a light sensor
  3. Pins 0, 1, 2 — general-purpose input/output pins for connecting sensors and actuators
  4. 3V pin — supplies power to connected components
  5. GND pin — ground; completes the circuit
  6. Touch logo — capacitive touch sensor; reacts to a finger without pressing
  7. Microphone LED — lights up whenever the microphone is active

Back

  1. Radio & Bluetooth antenna — printed onto the board; wireless communication with phones and other micro:bits
  2. Processor & temperature sensor — the nRF52833 chip that runs your program; also measures chip temperature
  3. Compass — detects magnetic fields and orientation
  4. Accelerometer — measures movement, tilt, and shake
  5. Pins — the full edge connector that slots into the Sensorbit
  6. Micro USB socket — power and program transfer from your laptop
  1. Battery socket — accepts a 2× AAA battery pack for portable use
  2. USB interface chip — a second, smaller processor that handles the USB connection and file transfer
  3. Speaker — plays tones and sounds directly
  4. Microphone — measures sound level; detects claps and shouts
  5. Red power LED — on whenever the board has power
  6. Yellow USB LED — flashes while a program is being written to the board
  7. Reset & power button — restarts your program; hold to power off
Note

Numbers 7 and 8 are skipped on the back: in the official numbering they refer to features of the older micro:bit V1, which our V2 boards replace with numbers 14 and 15.
