Microcontroller Programming and Interfacing for Medical and Agricultural Technology

Welcome to this intensive two-day course on microcontroller programming and interfacing. The course is designed for students with a medical or life-science background — no prior programming experience is assumed.

We use the BBC micro:bit and the Elecfreaks Smart Health Kit as our hardware platform, and MakeCode as our programming environment.

Course goals

By the end of this course you will be able to:

  1. Understand the process of ideation and innovation. Evaluate innovation cultures. Apply your knowledge by participating in brainstorming and team-building activities.
  2. Understand basic microcontroller architecture.
  3. Understand program flow, variables, loops, procedures, and querying peripherals with MakeCode.
  4. Understand common sensors and actuators. Evaluate and choose alternatives in the context of use cases.
  5. Understand the sensors and actuators provided with the Elecfreaks Smart Health Kit. Evaluate their utility and limitations.
  6. Apply your knowledge by connecting or activating peripherals. Write MakeCode under supervision. Evaluate your results and propose ways to improve.
  7. Create a prototype of a simple but potentially useful product. Evaluate the results and propose future improvements.

How this course works

This course follows a flipped, spaced-repetition format:

Exposure When What
First Before the course Watch the lecture videos and answer the Canvas quizzes
Second In-class discussion Revisit and deepen the same content together
Third Lab and project Apply the knowledge with real hardware

This means the pre-course lectures and quizzes are not optional — they are the foundation for everything that happens in the room.

Evening: Complete the lab quiz on Canvas. Watch videos about additional micro:bit kits (Heart and Brain SpikerBox, Spiker:Bit, Smart greenhouse). Complete the kit quizzes on Canvas.

Time Activity
9:30 Questions about additional micro:bit kits
10:00 Discuss and organise projects. Start work.
15:00 Shark tank: Present projects to teachers and students
17:00 Time to change the world.

Teams

You will work in five teams of four to five students. Teams are formed during the mixer on the evening of 16 June.

Each team will have access to one BBC micro:bit and one Elecfreaks Smart Health Kit, as well as share other kits with everyone for the group project on day 2.
